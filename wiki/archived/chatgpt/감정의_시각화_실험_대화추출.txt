[Paragraph 1: Context & Origin] This project emerges as an experimental side project branching from the graduation thesis, 'The Circulation of Emotion'. While the thesis explored the theoretical relationship between affect and urban space, this experiment shifts the focus to immediate interaction and computational visualization. We question: Can the invisible landscape of human emotion be constructed through the cold logic of code?

[Paragraph 2: The Paradox of AI] Ironically, Artificial Intelligence serves as the ideal partner for analyzing human affect. In a society where individuals often wear 'social masks' to hide their true feelings, AI remains blind to context. It reads only the raw signal. We initiated this project with a hypothesis: Perhaps AI, trained on the statistical averages of billions of human data points, is uniquely positioned to capture the essence of 'universal human emotion'—free from subjective distortion.

[Paragraph 3: From Micro to Macro] To prove this, we utilize two scales of bio-data. First, we translate facial expressions into a Micro-Sphere using computer vision. Second, we expand neural signals (EEG) into a Macro-Space using generative algorithms. This is an attempt to objectify the subjective, turning the fleeting moments of feeling into tangible, geometric forms.
