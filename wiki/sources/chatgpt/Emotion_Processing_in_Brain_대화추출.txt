**Stimulus Recognition and Limbic System Activation:**  
When an external stimulus enters the body, it is first recognized by the brain, particularly in areas such as the visual cortex. This process then activates the limbic system, the part of the brain responsible for processing emotions.

**Role of the Amygdala:**  
Within the limbic system, the amygdala functions as a “gateway of emotion.” It plays a crucial role in processing emotional responses, especially those related to fear.

**Release of Neurotransmitters and Hormones:**  

- **Neurotransmitters:** Neurotransmitters such as serotonin and norepinephrine are involved in regulating emotions. In particular, serotonin contributes to emotional stability and feelings of well-being.  
- **Hormones:** Hormones such as oxytocin—often released when people fall in love—play an important role in triggering emotional responses and related behaviors.

**Emotional Expression and Physical Responses:**  
Once emotions are generated, they manifest through specific facial expressions and bodily reactions, which occur under the direction of the brain.

**Sensory Input & Physiological Response**  
External stimuli are received through the sensory system, triggering internal bodily responses such as activation of the autonomic nervous system and hormonal changes.

**Interoceptive Information**  
Sensory input related to the body’s internal state—such as heart rate, blood pressure, and muscle tension—is continuously monitored and contributes to emotional processing.

**Cognitive Appraisal & Conceptualization**  
The stimulus and physiological responses are cognitively evaluated, and meaning is assigned using past experiences, memories, and conceptual frameworks.

**Sociocultural Context & Language**  
Social norms, linguistic expressions, and cultural codes shape how emotions are interpreted and expressed.

**Emotional Experience & Expression**  
As a result, individuals become aware of a particular emotional state and express it through facial expressions, language, and behavior.

**Feedback Loop**  
Emotional expressions and behaviors influence sensory input, bodily states, and cognitive processes again, creating a feedback loop that continuously reshapes emotional experience.
